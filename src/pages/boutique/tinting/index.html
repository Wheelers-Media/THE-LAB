Which shade is right?

Maximum privacy: 5% or 18% gives the darkest look and strongest privacy.

Daily-driver balance: 18% or 25% keeps the vehicle clean, dark, and usable.

Subtle upgrade: 35% adds comfort and UV protection without a heavily tinted look.

Factory match: Bring the vehicle in and we can match the front windows to existing rear privacy glass.

Shade vs. film type

Shade controls how dark the glass looks. Film type controls how it performs.

Carbon: privacy, appearance, UV protection, and everyday comfort.

Ceramic: higher heat rejection, better clarity, and premium cabin comfort.

Why Go Ceramic?
See Pricing →

Maximum Heat Rejection

Ceramic film rejects significantly more infrared heat than Carbon, keeping your cab cooler on BC summer days.

Better Clarity

Non-metallic ceramic construction means zero signal interference and crystal-clear visibility day or night.

Blocks Harmful UV Rays

Up to 99% UV rejection protects your skin and prevents your interior from fading and cracking over time.

Protects Interior from UV

Leather, dash, and upholstery all degrade from UV exposure. Ceramic film extends the life of your interior.

Less Glare

Reduce eye strain from sun and headlight glare, especially critical during long northern BC commutes.

Premium Comfort Every Drive

A cooler, glare-free cabin means more comfortable drives year-round — whether it's 35°C or a January blizzard.
